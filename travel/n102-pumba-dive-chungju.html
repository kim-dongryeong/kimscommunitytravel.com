[N102] Pumba Festival, K-pop Dive & Chungju

About This Trip

A summer festival weekend across three very different corners of Korea. Start with a hands-on village experience on Ganghwa Island (강화), then head to Eumseong (음성) for the country's most cheerful celebration — the Pumba Festival, honoring Korea's legendary wandering performers — followed by a free open-air K-pop night at the Chungju Dive Festival. Day two slows down along the Namhangang river in Chungju (충주): a thousand-year-old stone Buddha in a mountain pass, coffee, market food, Korea's newest aquarium, and a historic river port at golden hour. Government-sponsored for international students.

Destinations

🏝️
Doraemi Village
도래미마을 · Ganghwa
Day 1 · Morning

A rural experience village on Ganghwa Island whose name (島來美) means "a beautiful island you'll want to visit again." We join a hands-on village program with local residents — seasonal farming and traditional food-making, surrounded by rice paddies and tidal flats.

Village website
⛩️
Mitasa Temple
미타사 · Eumseong
Day 1 · Afternoon

A thousand-year temple first founded by the monk Wonhyo in the 7th century. Its skyline is unforgettable: a 41-meter golden statue of Jijang Bosal — 108 cheok tall, echoing the 108 worldly desires — said to be the largest of its kind in East Asia. A short trekking course climbs past a Goryeo-era Buddha carved into the rock face.

Eumseong Culture Encyclopedia
🥁
Eumseong Pumba Festival
음성품바축제 · Eumseong
Day 1 · Evening

Korea's most cheerful festival — a celebration of the pumba, the wandering beggar-performers of old Korea, and of Choi Gwi-dong, the "beggar saint" of Eumseong who sheltered the poor. Expect raucous song-and-drum street performances, festival food alleys, and the grand evening parade through the festival grounds. We receive ₩10,000 in local gift certificates each — dinner is on the festival stalls.

VisitKorea
🎤
Chungju Dive Festival
충주 다이브 페스티벌 · Chungju
Day 1 · Night

Chungju's open-air summer K-culture festival. We arrive for the "DIVE into the K-pop" night — a free outdoor concert with OH MY GIRL, STAYC, SAY MY NAME and more taking the stage. Dance until check-in time.

🗿
Mireukdaewonji Temple Site
충주 미륵대원지 · Chungju
Day 2 · Morning

The stone remains of a Goryeo-era temple complex high in the Haneuljae pass — Historic Site No. 317. Legend says Mauitaeja, the last prince of Silla, founded it on his sorrowful walk to the Geumgang Mountains. Its stone Maitreya Buddha has watched over the pass for a thousand years. We listen to gugak — traditional Korean music — amid the ruins.

VisitKorea
☕
Chungju Coffee Museum
충주커피박물관 · Chungju
Day 2 · Late Morning

A garden museum of world coffee culture — antique grinders, roasters and curiosities collected from around the globe, plus a roasting and barista experience center. A complimentary café drink is included.

Museum website
🍜
Chungju Market
충주시장 · Chungju
Day 2 · Lunch

Free time for lunch in Chungju's traditional market — noodle stalls, street-food alleys, mountains of produce, and the kind of ajumma hospitality no food court can match.

🐟
Chungju Aquarium
충주 아쿠아리움 · Chungju
Day 2 · Afternoon

Korea's newest aquarium, opened in late 2025 beside Tangeum Lake — 73 species and some 2,400 creatures with a focus on freshwater life, from sturgeon to the native stream fish of the Namhangang.

🌊
Mokgye Naru River Port
목계나루 · Chungju
Day 2 · Late Afternoon

Once the largest inland river port on the Namhangang — in its Joseon-era heyday, home to 800 households and a hundred merchant ships running goods to Seoul. Immortalized in Shin Gyeong-rim's beloved poem "Mokgye Market," it is now a quiet riverside cultural space, and our last golden-hour stop before the bus home.
